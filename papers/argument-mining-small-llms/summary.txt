Leveraging Small LLMs for Argument Mining in Education: Argument Component
Identification, Classification, and Assessment
Lucile Favero, Juan Antonio Perez-Ortiz, Tanja Kaser, Nuria Oliver.
arXiv:2502.14389 (v2, Nov 2025). ELLIS Alicante / Univ. Alacant / EPFL.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IT DOES:
Tests whether small, local, open-source LLMs can do the full argument-mining pipeline
on student essays: (1) SEGMENT the essay into argument components, (2) CLASSIFY each
into 7 types (Lead, Position, Claim, Counterclaim, Rebuttal, Evidence, Concluding
Statement), (3) ASSESS quality (Ineffective/Adequate/Effective). Few-shot prompting
vs LoRA/QLoRA fine-tuning; compared to encoder baselines and GPT-4o-mini.

KEY FINDINGS:
- Data: Kaggle "Feedback Prize - Effectiveness" (PERSUADE 2.0 subset), grade 6-12 US
  essays; test set 419 essays / 3,711 arguments. Models: Qwen2.5-7B, Llama3.1-8B,
  Gemma2-9B.
- Segmentation: best Llama3.1-8B fine-tuned macro-F1 = 87.52 (+18 pts over Longformer).
- Type classification: 79.74 F1 with GOLD segmentation, but only 51.45 F1 with the
  model's own INFERRED segmentation. Quality assessment weak (~44.56 F1) for everyone.
- Per-type F1 (inferred seg): Concluding 74.4, Evidence 67.1, Lead 64.6 (high recall/
  low precision -> over-labels), Position 55.6, Claim 51.8, Counterclaim 50.7,
  Rebuttal 47.5. Best at conclusions/evidence; worst at rebuttals/counterclaims.
- SEGMENTATION IS THE BOTTLENECK: B-token (component start) precision only 66%, and
  boundary errors cascade -- many Claims/Evidence are lost to the "Echec" mismatch
  column, dragging down classification.
- Prompts are role-based ("You are a strict AI evaluator specializing in detecting the
  type of argument components...") with a token-insertion schema (<SEP>, then
  <Type, Quality> labels) rather than free prose. Fine-tuning >> few-shot for seg/type.

RELEVANCE TO THE PROJECT:
- Q3 (citing components) -- PRIMARY. Direct evidence on how well an LLM identifies/cites
  essay components: good in the gold-segmentation regime (~0.80), but ~0.51 end-to-end.
- Q4 (MLA parser) -- PRIMARY justification. Because segmentation is the dominant error
  source, supplying CLEAN, pre-segmented structure (what your MLA parser would do) should
  move classification from the ~0.51 (inferred) regime toward the ~0.80 (gold) regime.
  This is the single strongest argument in the set for why the parser matters.
- Q2 (schema/role) -- secondary: the role-based prompt + token schema mirror your design.

CAVEATS:
- English high-school writing only (PERSUADE); generalization unknown.
- Quality-assessment ceiling is low for all methods, partly due to noisy annotations.
